Introduction to Artificial Intelligence

Published on: March 8, 2026 by Prince Gupta

Artificial Intelligence (AI) is a rapidly evolving field of computer science dedicated to creating machines that can reason, learn, and act autonomously. It encompasses a broad range of technologies, from simple rule-based systems to complex neural networks capable of deep learning.

"AI is not just about making computers smarter, but about making them more useful to humanity." — Sundar Pichai

The potential of AI is immense, promising to transform every sector from healthcare and finance to education and entertainment. We are moving from a world where computers merely process data to one where they understand and interpret it.

Types of AI

Narrow AI (Weak AI)

Designed for specific tasks, like image recognition or playing chess. It cannot perform beyond its programmed functions.

General AI (Strong AI)

Hypothetical AI that can understand, learn, and apply intelligence to any intellectual task that a human being can.

Superintelligence

An AI that surpasses human intellect in virtually every field, including scientific creativity, general wisdom, and social skills.

Modern AI Applications

Healthcare Diagnostics

AI assists doctors in diagnosing diseases with higher accuracy and speed, from MRI scans to genetic analysis.

Autonomous Vehicles

Self-driving cars use AI for perception, decision-making, and navigation. It's a complex system that reduces human error aims for safer roads.

Natural Language Processing (NLP)

Powers virtual assistants, translation services, and sentiment analysis tools. Learn more about NLP.

A Brief History of AI

  • 1950s - The Dawn of AI

    Alan Turing proposes the Turing Test. John McCarthy coins the term "Artificial Intelligence."

  • 1980s - Expert Systems

    Focus shifts to knowledge-based systems for specific domains. LISP machines gain popularity.

  • 2010s - Deep Learning Revolution

    Breakthroughs in neural networks lead to significant advances in image and speech recognition.

  • 2020s - Generative AI & LLMs

    Large Language Models (LLMs) and generative AI begin to transform content creation and interaction.

Performance Metrics: Understanding TGS

To evaluate the efficiency of large language models and other generative AI, metrics like TGS (Tokens per GPU per Second) are crucial. A higher TGS indicates faster generation and better resource utilization.

Understanding the Formula

The TGS for a given inference run can be approximated by:


TGS = (total_output_tokens / num_gpus) / inference_time_seconds

# Example calculation
total_output_tokens = 10000
num_gpus = 4
inference_time_seconds = 5

calculated_tgs = (total_output_tokens / num_gpus) / inference_time_seconds
print(f"Calculated TGS: {calculated_tgs}") # Output: Calculated TGS: 500.0

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between AI and Machine Learning?
Machine Learning (ML) is a subset of AI that uses statistical methods to enable machines to improve with experience. AI is the broader concept of machines exhibiting intelligence.
Is AI going to take over human jobs?
While AI will automate some tasks and change job descriptions, it's also expected to create new jobs and enhance human capabilities, leading to a shift in the job market.
How does AI learn?
AI learns through algorithms that process vast amounts of data, identify patterns, and make predictions or decisions. This process is often called training.
